Why VEX Robotics Matters

VEX Robotics is more than a robot competition. It’s a hands-on, team-based way for students to learn engineering, coding, communication, and leadership—skills that directly connect to college, careers, and the growing technology economy.

For Students

  • Build and program real robots to play a yearly global game.
  • Practice problem-solving, strategy, and sportsmanship.
  • Earn recognition at local leagues, state events, and world championships.

For Families & Volunteers

  • Be on the front row as your student learns and competes.
  • Help with events, travel, team coordination, or notebooks—no experience required.
  • Support a positive, structured after-school environment.

For Local Companies

  • Invest directly in a STEM talent pipeline in southern West Virginia.
  • Offer sponsorships, in-kind donations, or mentoring.
  • Connect your brand with innovation, education, and community impact.

Mercer County’s Robotics Impact

Mercer County Schools and local clubs have grown into a recognized robotics community, with students competing in county leagues, state championships, and even world-level events.

County-Wide VEX IQ League

Mercer County runs a blended VEX IQ league with league nights at local schools, a county championship, and open qualifiers that bring together elementary and middle school teams.

State & World Championship Trips

Mercer County teams have earned spots at the West Virginia State Championship and at the VEX Robotics World Championship in Dallas, TX—showing that local students can compete on the global stage.

4-H & Community Teams

In addition to school teams, community organizations and 4-H robotics clubs give students additional chances to build, practice, and compete using VEX technology.

Pathways to College & Careers

Robotics experience helps students stand out on college applications and prepares them for careers in engineering, automation, manufacturing, computer science, and more.

How You Can Get Involved

VEX Robotics thrives when families, educators, and local businesses pull together. Here are concrete ways you can make a difference for Mercer County teams.

For Families

  • Ask your student’s school about existing VEX teams or how to start one.
  • Volunteer to help with transportation, snacks, event setup, or team coordination.
  • Encourage your student to stick with the season—growth happens over time.

For Volunteers

  • Serve as an event volunteer (queuing, scoring, field reset, hospitality).
  • Help students with notebooks, communication, or project presentations.
  • Mentor a team in areas like coding, engineering design, or teamwork.

For Local Companies

  • Provide financial sponsorships to offset parts, registration, and travel costs.
  • Offer in-kind support: T-shirts, banners, tools, machining, or meeting space.
  • Visit events to see the impact, then partner with teams for ongoing STEM outreach.
